Definitions core
Auto-correlation (WSS) Rxx(τ) = E[x(t) x(t+τ)]Expectation of product shifted by τ
Power Spectral Density Sxx(f) = ℱ{Rxx(τ)}Fourier transform of autocorrelation (Wiener–Khinchin)
Cross-correlation Rxy(τ) = E[x(t) y(t+τ)]
Cross-spectral density Sxy(f) = ℱ{Rxy(τ)}
Total average power Px = Rxx(0) = ∫Sxx(f) dfIntegrate PSD over all frequencies
Input–Output PSD Relations key results
Output PSD Syy(f) = |H(f)|² · Sxx(f)|H(f)|² is the power transfer function
Cross-PSD (input→output) Sxy(f) = H(f) · Sxx(f)Complex — retains phase of H(f)
Cross-PSD (output→input) Syx(f) = H*(f) · Sxx(f)H*(f) = complex conjugate of H(f)
Output autocorrelation Ryy(τ) = h(τ) ∗ h(-τ) ∗ Rxx(τ)Convolution with mirrored impulse response
Output cross-correlation Rxy(τ) = h(τ) ∗ Rxx(τ)Input–output cross-corr = h ∗ Rxx
Properties of Sxx(f) properties
Non-negativity Sxx(f) ≥ 0 ∀ fPSD is always real and non-negative
Real-valued Sxx(f) ∈ ℝEven though Rxx(τ) may be complex for complex signals
Even symmetry (real signals) Sxx(-f) = Sxx(f)Holds when x(t) is a real-valued signal
Conjugate symmetry (general) Sxx(-f) = Sxx*(f)General case; reduces to even if x(t) ∈ ℝ
Parseval / power link Rxx(0) = ∫-∞+∞ Sxx(f) dfTotal power = area under PSD curve
Properties of Sxy(f) cross-spectral
Conjugate symmetry Sxy(-f) = Sxy*(f)For real jointly-WSS signals
Hermitian relation Syx(f) = Sxy*(f)Swapping order conjugates the CSD
Schwarz inequality |Sxy(f)|² ≤ Sxx(f) · Syy(f)Upper-bounds cross-spectral magnitude
Generally complex Sxy(f) = |Sxy(f)| ejφ(f)Has both magnitude and phase (unlike PSD)
Coherence function γ²xy(f) = |Sxy(f)|² / [Sxx(f)·Syy(f)]0 ≤ γ² ≤ 1; measures linear coupling
Special Cases & Common Signals examples
White noise input Sxx(f) = N₀/2 (flat)Uncorrelated — Rxx(τ) = (N₀/2)δ(τ)
White noise → LTI output Syy(f) = (N₀/2) |H(f)|²Output PSD shaped entirely by |H(f)|²
Ideal bandpass filter Py = (N₀/2) · 2BB = one-sided bandwidth; power = N₀B
Sinusoid x(t)=A cos(2πf₀t+φ) Sxx(f) = (A²/4)[δ(f-f₀)+δ(f+f₀)]Power concentrated at ±f₀

Bandwidth & Power Measures measures
Band power P[f₁,f₂] = ∫f₁f₂ Sxx(f) dfPower in a specific frequency band (one-sided)
Noise equivalent BW BN = ∫|H(f)|² df / |H(f₀)|²maxEquivalent brick-wall bandwidth of H(f)
RMS bandwidth Brms = √[ ∫f²S(f)df / ∫S(f)df ]Second moment of PSD (spectral spread)
SNR in bandwidth B SNR = Psignal / (N₀ · B)Signal power divided by noise power in B
